# SWOT Analysis

A strategic framework to evaluate a company's internal strengths/weaknesses
and external opportunities/threats.

## The Four Quadrants

| Factor | Type | Key Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Strengths** | Internal / Positive | What does the company do better than competitors? |
| **Weaknesses** | Internal / Negative | Where does the company underperform or lack resources? |
| **Opportunities** | External / Positive | What market trends or gaps can the company exploit? |
| **Threats** | External / Negative | What external forces could harm the business? |

## Example (Apple)

| | Positive | Negative |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | Strong brand, ecosystem lock-in | High product prices, reliance on iPhone revenue |
| **External** | Wearables/AI growth markets | Intense competition, regulatory scrutiny |

## Key Takeaways

- Strengths + Opportunities → double down and invest
- Strengths + Threats → use strengths to defend against threats
- Weaknesses + Opportunities → fix gaps to capture upside
- Weaknesses + Threats → highest risk, needs immediate attention

## Limitations

- Subjective — depends on who fills it out
- Static snapshot, not forward-looking
- Doesn't prioritize factors by importance

## Best Used With

- Porter's Five Forces (external competitive context)
- DuPont Analysis (quantitative financial backing)
