# DuPont Analysis

A framework to decompose **Return on Equity (ROE)** into its underlying drivers.
Originally developed at DuPont Corporation in the 1920s.

## 3-Factor Model (Classic)

ROE = Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier

| Component | Formula | Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Net Profit Margin** | Net Income / Revenue | Operating efficiency |
| **Asset Turnover** | Revenue / Total Assets | Asset efficiency |
| **Equity Multiplier** | Total Assets / Equity | Financial leverage |

## 5-Factor Model (Advanced)

ROE = Tax Burden × Interest Burden × Operating Margin × Asset Turnover × Financial Leverage

| Component | Formula | Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Tax Burden** | Net Income / Pre-Tax Income | Profit surviving taxation |
| **Interest Burden** | Pre-Tax Income / EBIT | Cost of debt drag |
| **Operating Margin** | EBIT / Revenue | Core profitability |
| **Asset Turnover** | Revenue / Assets | Capital efficiency |
| **Financial Leverage** | Assets / Equity | Debt amplification |

## Key Takeaways

- Two companies with the same ROE can arrive there very differently
- High ROE driven by **margins/efficiency** = sustainable
- High ROE driven by **leverage** = riskier
- Best used for peer comparisons and tracking trends over time

## Limitations

- Backward-looking (historical data only)
- Can be distorted by one-time items in net income
- Leverage-inflated ROE may look attractive but masks risk
