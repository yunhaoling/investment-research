    Porter's Five Forces

    A framework by Michael Porter (1979) to analyze industry competitiveness and long-term profitability.

    The Five Forces

    Force High Threat When…
    Competitive Rivalry Many competitors, slow growth, commodity products
    Threat of New Entrants Low capital barriers, weak brand loyalty, few regulations
    Supplier Power Few suppliers, unique inputs, high switching costs
    Buyer Power Many alternatives, low switching costs, price-sensitive customers
    Threat of Substitutes Cheaper or better alternatives exist outside the industry

    Key Takeaways

    • Attractive industries have all five forces weak, enabling sustained above-normal profits
    • Companies should position where forces are weakest and shape industry structure to their advantage
    • Maps directly to the concept of an economic moat in equity investing

    Limitations

    • Static snapshot — doesn't capture rapid disruption well
    • Assumes clear industry boundaries (blurry in tech)
    • Doesn't account for complementary products/ecosystems

    Best Used With

    • SWOT Analysis
    • DuPont Analysis
    • Competitive benchmarking
